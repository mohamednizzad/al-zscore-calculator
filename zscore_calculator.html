How the Z-score is calculated

The formula
Z = (x − μ) ÷ σ
Final Z-score = (Z₁ + Z₂ + Z₃) ÷ 3
x — your raw mark in a subject (0–100)
μ — island-wide mean (average) mark for that subject
σ — standard deviation of all candidates' marks for that subject
1

Sit three A/L subjects

Every candidate sits three subjects within their stream (e.g. Physics, Chemistry, Combined Maths for Physical Science). Each paper is marked out of 100.

2

UGC computes the island-wide mean and standard deviation per subject

After all scripts are marked, the Department of Examinations calculates the mean (μ) and standard deviation (σ) for every subject separately. This accounts for difficulty differences between papers.

3

Calculate Z for each subject

Subtract the subject mean from your raw mark, then divide by the standard deviation: Z = (x − μ) ÷ σ. A positive Z means you scored above average; negative means below. The result is typically between −3 and +3.

4

Average the three subject Z-scores

The final Z-score is the simple average: (Z₁ + Z₂ + Z₃) ÷ 3. This single number is what the UGC uses to rank all candidates island-wide.

5

District quota is applied on top

40% of university seats are filled by island-wide Z-score rank. The remaining 55% are allocated by district, proportional to population. An additional 5% goes to students from 13 educationally disadvantaged districts.
